Computational Thinking (Ordinary Level)

📌 What is Computational Thinking?

Computational Thinking is a way of solving problems step-by-step so they can be solved by a computer.

🧠 The 4 Steps

1. Decomposition

Breaking a big problem into smaller parts.

2. Pattern Recognition

Finding similarities in problems.

3. Abstraction

Ignoring unimportant details.

4. Algorithm

A step-by-step plan to solve a problem.

💡 Example

Making a game:

  • Split into parts (controls, score)
  • Look for patterns
  • Ignore background details
  • Write steps for gameplay

⭐ Why it is important

  • Helps solve problems
  • Makes coding easier
  • Used in games and apps

🧪 Exam Questions

Q1: What is computational thinking? (3 marks)

Answer: A way of solving problems step-by-step so a computer can understand them.

Q2: What is decomposition? (3 marks)

Answer: Breaking a problem into smaller parts.

Q3: Give one example of abstraction. (3 marks)

Answer: Ignoring background details in a game.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Learn the 4 steps (very common question)
  • Keep answers short and simple
  • Use everyday examples (games, apps)
  • Focus on understanding, not technical words
