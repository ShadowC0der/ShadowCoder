Computational Thinking (Higher Level)

📌 What is Computational Thinking?

Computational Thinking is a problem-solving process that involves breaking down problems and creating step-by-step solutions that can be solved by a computer.

🧠 Four Pillars of Computational Thinking

1. Decomposition

Breaking a large problem into smaller parts.

2. Pattern Recognition

Finding similarities between problems.

3. Abstraction

Ignoring unnecessary details to focus on important information.

4. Algorithm Design

Creating step-by-step instructions to solve a problem.

💡 Example

Building a game:

  • Decomposition → split into graphics, controls, scoring
  • Pattern recognition → reuse movement systems
  • Abstraction → ignore background details
  • Algorithm → write rules for gameplay

⭐ Why Computational Thinking is Important

  • Helps solve complex problems
  • Makes programming easier
  • Used in real-world systems like AI and apps

🧪 Exam Questions

Q1: Define computational thinking. (3 marks)

Answer: A problem-solving process that breaks problems into steps that can be solved by a computer.

Q2: Explain decomposition. (3 marks)

Answer: Breaking a large problem into smaller, more manageable parts.

Q3: Describe abstraction. (4 marks)

Answer: Removing unnecessary details to focus only on important information.
