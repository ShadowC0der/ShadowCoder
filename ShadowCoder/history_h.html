History of Computers (Higher Level)

📌 Overview

Computers have evolved through different generations, becoming smaller, faster, and more powerful over time.

🧠 Generations of Computers

1st Generation (1940–1950s)

  • Used vacuum tubes
  • Very large and slow
  • Produced lots of heat

2nd Generation (1950–1960s)

  • Used transistors
  • Smaller and faster than 1st gen

3rd Generation (1960–1970s)

  • Used integrated circuits (ICs)
  • More reliable and efficient

4th Generation (1970–present)

  • Microprocessors used
  • Personal computers introduced
  • Very fast and small

⚡ Improvements Over Time

  • Smaller size
  • Faster processing speed
  • Less heat produced
  • Increased storage

💡 Example Question

Explain the difference between 1st and 4th generation computers.

1st generation computers were large, slow, and used vacuum tubes. 4th generation computers are small, fast, and use microprocessors.

🧪 Exam Questions

Q1: What is a generation of computers? (3 marks)

Answer: A stage in the development of computers based on technology used.

Q2: Name one feature of second generation computers. (3 marks)

Answer: They used transistors.

Q3: Give two improvements in modern computers. (4 marks)

Answer: Faster processing speed and smaller size.

🎯 Exam Tips

  • Learn the 4 generations in order
  • Memorise key technology (vacuum tubes → transistors → ICs → microprocessors)
  • Use comparison questions (very common)
  • Keep answers structured and short
